Snap-Points can be used as preferred landing points for the content. When the user pans in the element, the panning motion stops or ‘snaps to’ the nearest snap-point, based on the snap-point type and inertia. There are two different types of snap-points:

  • Mandatory Snap-Points - When the user lifts their finger, the panned content is always smoothly animated to a snap-point.
  • Proximity Snap-Points - Unlike mandatory snap-points, the panned content comes to a full stop if the motion naturally ends "close enough" to a snap-point.
